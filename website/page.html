Predicting ridership impacts of new bike infrastructure

Data Information
  • The ridership data and bike-station data used in this project are from NYC DOT.
  • The road data used in this project are from LION.
  • Half of the data in August, 2020 are aggregrated to be visualized in this website.
  • Our study area comprises a half mile buffer around the Citibike stations that were available in August of 2020. This study area extends for most of Manhattan, and parts of the Bronx, Brooklyn, and Queens.
  • The predictions under the Scenario part are based on random-forest model.

    • For more information about the project, please check the GitHub link at the bottom.
Instructions
Project Goals
This is the project for MUSA practium, in year 2021.
The goal of the project is to predict the impacts of new bikelanes on bike riderships.
Predicting the impact of a new bikelane on a particular street would give our client, the NYC DOT, and other users an additional tool to use in deciding which streets to prioritize for bike lanes.

Page Instructions
  • Let's Visualize
    • The choice of ridership is set as "checked" as default. By clicking the button, we are able to visualize the bike riderships over our study area.
    • Similarly, clicking buttons of bikelanes and bike stations enables us to see the bike lanes and bike stations over study area.
    • Clicking on each road segment, we are able to see the actual bike trips and the predicted biketrips.
  • Scenarios
    • There are three different scenarios. Moreover, under each scenarios, choosing "Protected Bikelanes" visualizes predicted changes of bike trips from changing the roads to proteced bikelanes; alternatively, clicking "Unproteced Bikelanes" visualizes predicted changes of bike trips from changing the roads to unprotected bikelanes.
    • There is only one choice, "Protected Bikelanes", under the Upper Manhattan scenario because around half of bikelanes under this scenario are already unprotected bikelanes. Therefore, only predictions of Protected Bikelanes are provided.
    • Clicking on each road segment, we are able to see the actual bike trips and the predicted biketrips.
Notifications
  • The data included in this website should not be misused.
  • The data included in this website should not be used for economic purpose without notifying members of NYC DOT.
