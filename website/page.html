Cycling Infrastructure Scenario Planning in NYC

Data Information
  • The ridership data and bike-station data used in this project are from NYC DOT.
  • The road data used in this project are from LION.
  • Half of the data in August, 2020 are aggregrated to be visualized in this website.
  • Our study area comprises a half mile buffer around the Citibike stations that were available in August of 2020. This study area extends for most of Manhattan, and parts of the Bronx, Brooklyn, and Queens.
  • The predictions under the Scenario part are based on random-forest model.

    • For more information about the project, please check the GitHub link at the bottom.
Instructions
Project Goals
This is the project for MUSA practium, in year 2021.
The goal of the project is to predict the impacts of new bike lanes on bike ridership.
Predicting the impact of a new bikelane on a particular street would help our client, NYC DOT, and other users prioritize streets for cycling infrastructure investments.

Page Instructions
  • Let's Visualize
    • The choice of ridership is set as "checked" as default. By clicking the button, we are able to visualize the CitiBike ridership over our study area.
    • Similarly, clicking the buttons for bike lanes and bike stations enables us to see the bike lanes and CitiBike stations throughout the study area.
    • Clicking on each road segment, we are able to see the count of bike trips from our data, representing an total monthly count for August 2020 for a subset of CitiBike trips.
  • Scenarios
    • There are three different scenarios. Moreover, under each scenarios, choosing "Protected Bike Lanes" visualizes predicted changes of bike trips from changing the roads to proteced bike lanes; alternatively, clicking "Unproteced Bike Lanes" visualizes predicted changes of bike trips from changing the roads to unprotected bike lanes.
    • There is only one choice, "Protected Bike Lanes", under the Upper Manhattan scenario because around half of bike lanes under this scenario are already unprotected bike lanes. Therefore, only predictions of Protected Bike Lanes are provided.
    • Clicking on each road segment, we are able to see the observed bike trips and the predicted biketrips.
Notifications
